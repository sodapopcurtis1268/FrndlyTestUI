Analysis & Recommendations

🔍 Key Trends

  • Live Feed drops = 37.5% of all incidents. Weigel (4×), MeTV group (3×), and low-bitrate feeds are repeat offenders.
  • Sept 2024 was the worst month: 5 incidents in 6 days — all live feed related.
  • Release days are high-risk: 3 of 7 App Crash incidents are release-day regressions (Vizio, Roku, Multi-platform).
  • Monitoring is reactive: SSL cert, ad zeros, EPG staleness, and feed drops all discovered by users before any alert fired.
  • Third-party deps caused 6+ incidents: Bitmovin SDK, Akamai CDN, Gracenote, TiVo integration.

⚠️ Device Risk

  • Vizio appears in 3 separate themes (App Crashes, EPG/CMS, Search) — highest cross-theme risk platform.
  • Samsung issues are SDK-driven (Bitmovin v8.228) — controllable with version pinning.
  • 25 of 40 incidents affect all platforms simultaneously — these are backend/feed issues, not device bugs.
  • Fire TV, Roku, and iOS each have 1 device-specific incident — manageable with platform regression gates.

🛠️ Top 5 Improvements

  • #1 Deploy automated feed bitrate + drop alerting for all live channels.
  • #2 Enforce mandatory device-matrix smoke test gate before every release.
  • #3 Build single monitoring dashboard: feeds, ads, EPG freshness, SSL, search index.
  • #4 Pin third-party SDK versions; require staging validation before any upgrade ships.
  • #5 Standardize RCA template with 48-hour SLA for Blocker incidents.

📋 Infrastructure Risk

  • 100% Blocker rate — all 3 infrastructure incidents were Blockers. Highest severity theme.
  • SSL cert propagated to India but not US — caused 80-minute outage. Multi-region validation checklist needed.
  • DB upgrade triggered platform-wide logout. Change management process needed for infra changes.
  • No Akamai alerting in place when CDN disrupted 4 platforms simultaneously (July 2025).
